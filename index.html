Functional Neurological Disorder & Trauma: Evidence-Based Clinical Insights

An interactive resource synthesizing current knowledge on FND diagnosis, the significant role of trauma and comorbidities, and integrated, evidence-based treatment strategies. Designed for clinicians and advanced students.

FND Essentials

Diagnosis is POSITIVE, Not Exclusionary[1]
Relies on specific neurological signs inconsistent with known disease patterns.
Role of Stressors & Maltreatment
Common risk factors (~3x higher rate vs controls)[3]. Emotional Neglect strongest childhood link (OR 5.6)[3]. BUT: Not required for diagnosis.[1, 3]
Affects brain function ('software'), not structure ('hardware').[1, 2]

Click Cards to Reveal Facts:

Myth: Faking Symptoms
Fact: Involuntary symptoms from altered brain function.[1, 2]
Myth: Single Trauma Cause
Fact: Trauma/stress common, but not required. Dx via positive signs.[3, 1]
Myth: Abuse is Main Risk
Fact: Emotional Neglect has *strongest* childhood link (OR 5.6).[3]
Myth: Dx of Exclusion
Fact: Made via *positive* neurological findings.[1, 2]

The Comorbidity Landscape

Almost All Have ≥1 Psychiatric Comorbidity[5]

Conditions often form a complex clinical *cluster*, suggesting foundational links and requiring integrated care.[5]

Common Comorbidities (Hover for Info):

  • PTSD Common (esp. Trauma Subtype[4]). Linked to ↑severity, ↓health, dissociation. Needs targeted Tx.[5, 4]
  • Mood Disorders Depression common, impacts QoL. Treat concurrently.[1, 2]
  • Anxiety Disorders GAD, Panic common. Can exacerbate symptoms. Address with CBT/meds.[1, 2]
  • BPD Features Emotional dysregulation impacts FND. Consider DBT principles.[5]
  • ASD Features Potential overlap. Sensory issues key in rehab. Adapt communication.[5]
  • Alexithymia Difficulty ID'ing emotions impacts therapy. Needs specific focus.[4, 5]

Conceptualizing FND: Biopsychosocial Factors

Understanding FND requires considering interacting biological, psychological, and social factors. Explore key elements below.

Biological Factors

  • Focus on altered brain **function** ('software'), not primary structure.[1, 2]
  • Altered network connectivity & processing.
  • Role of Neurosensitivity (heightened reactivity).[5]
  • Potential influence of early neurodevelopment.[5]
  • Hypothesized role of neuroinflammation post-trauma.[5]

Key Brain Area Functions Altered:

  • SMA: Motor planning/initiation deficits[1]
  • Thalamus: Sensory gating/relay issues[1]
  • Amygdala: Emotion/threat processing alterations, esp. w/ trauma[4]
  • Insula: Interoception/self-awareness/dissociation link[4]

Psychological Factors

  • Alexithymia: Difficulty identifying/expressing emotions impacts processing, therapy.[4, 5]
  • Dissociation: Common, especially with PNES presentation.[5]
  • Coping Styles: Avoidance, safety behaviors can maintain symptoms.[1]
  • Trauma History: PTSD symptoms, trauma-related cognitions/schemas.[4]
  • Personality Traits: BPD features (emotional dysregulation) relevant.[5]
  • Emotional Dysregulation: Central mechanism linking stress to somatic symptoms.[5]

Social & Environmental Factors

  • Early Life Adversity: Esp. Emotional Neglect (high OR)[3], Abuse.[3]
  • Interpersonal Trauma History: Beyond childhood.[5]
  • Current Life Stressors: Can precipitate or exacerbate FND.[3, 5]
  • Social Support: Quality impacts coping and outcomes.
  • Cultural Factors: Influence symptom expression and illness beliefs.
  • Stigma: Can hinder help-seeking and recovery.[5 discussion]

Reference Model: Proposed Comorbidity Pathways

Crafting an Integrated & Tailored Treatment Plan

Effective FND treatment requires flexibility and synergy between modalities. Collaboration across disciplines is paramount.[1, 2]

Foundational Pillars

  • Positive Diagnosis & Psychoeducation: Essential first step for alliance. Validate symptoms.[1, 2]
  • Multidisciplinary Team Activation: Define roles (Neuro, Psych, PT, OT, SLP). Coordinate care.[1, 2]
  • Set Patient-Centered Goals: Focus on functional targets.

Rehabilitation Strategies

  • PT/OT/SLP: Use distraction, automatic movement, ADL strategies. Adapt for sensory profile.[1, 2]
Rehab progress informs psych pacing & vice-versa. Requires team communication.

Psychological Therapies

  • CBT (Core): Target unhelpful thoughts, avoidance, safety behaviors. Stress management.[1, 2]
  • Other Modalities: Hypnosis (Motor FND evidence[1]), Mindfulness/ACT (Emotion Reg[4]).
Address alexithymia if present. Coping skills support rehab engagement.

Addressing Trauma

Identifying Trauma Impact & "Subtype"
Severity[4]
Phys Health[4]
Circuit Impact[4]

Assess PTSD & Maltreatment (esp. Neglect[3]). Presence linked to worse outcomes ("Trauma Subtype" heuristic).[4]

Trauma-Focused Treatment
  • Stabilization First: Essential before processing.
  • Options (Patient Ready): PE, EMDR, CPT, Psychodynamic, Sensorimotor.[1, 4]
Requires careful timing & close team coordination to manage potential destabilization.

Managing Comorbidities

  • BPD: Consider DBT principles for emotional regulation.[5]
  • ASD: Adapt communication & sensory environment.[5]
  • Alexithymia: Specific focus on emotion ID/expression needed.[4, 5]
  • Mood/Anxiety: Treat concurrently (SSRIs, therapy).[1, 2]
  • Pharmacotherapy: For *comorbidities*, not FND itself.[1, 2]
Treating underlying comorbidities directly supports FND recovery.

Integrating the Approach: Key Considerations

  • Primary FND Symptom Type(s)?
  • Trauma Profile & "Subtype"? Readiness?
  • Key Comorbidities Impacting Care (BPD, ASD, Alexithymia)?
  • Therapeutic Alliance & Motivation?
  • Social Context (Support, Stressors, Culture)?
  • Relevant Prognostic Factors?[1]
  • Need for Team Communication?
